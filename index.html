I'm a journalist who writes about technology, history, and science. I read the privacy policies so you don't have to.

Right now I'm a Senior Reporter at Gizmodo. I've covered privacy, algorithms, AI, and social media for more than five years.

My work has sparked investigations by Congress and the FTC and changed American privacy law. After my reporting launched an FTC enforcement action, Facebook disabled its facial recognition system and deleted face data from over a billion people.

I've appeared on CBS Mornings, the Today show, NBC News, and BBC Radio. Let me know if you need someone to talk tech.

I'm also a video and podcast producer. I'm working on an upcoming weekly video series for Gizmodo, and I hosted the short-lived Consumer Reports podcast. In my spare time, I make music and sound art and fix typos on this website.
